Implementing Guidelines for the GORDON COLLEGE SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM
(Excerpts from Sangguniang Panlungsod Resolution No. 111 Series of 2012)

As a scholar, we would like to formalize the following Implementing Guidelines covering your scholarship grant as follows:

1. The City Scholarship Program-Tertiary Level shall cover only four years (8 semesters) of financial assistance.
2. The scholarship grant shall consist of (FULL / PARTIAL) Tuition Fee / Miscellaneous Fee Exemption depending on the type of Scholarship.
3. This scholarship grant is subject to per semester review of your academic performance / achievement.
4. To be able to retain the scholarship grant, all the scholars must meet the following conditions every semester

a. Maintain a semestral grade:
iii. Achievement Scholarship ( Culture & Arts, Varsity, Olongapo City Marching Band)
1. Scholars must have a GPA of at least 75%, with no failing grade.
2. Give significant contribution to the College / or the City.

b. Must enroll in at least 18 units unless otherwise specified by the curriculum and shall finish his / her course within the prescribed course duration.
5. The scholarship grant shall automatically be forfeited in case of expulsion from the school or if the scholar discontinues his / her studies.
6. A written reprimand issued by the Office of Student Welfare and Services and / or Scholar's Affairs Office for the minor offenses or a major offense shall mean termination of the scholarship program.
7. Dropping out, deferment or non-enrolment for the next semester shall terminate the program and shall oblige the student to reimburse all amount spent on scholarship. However if a leave of absence is due to unavoidable circumstance like illness or accident, scholarship shall be continued upon return to school provided that the proper document shall be presented.
8. The scholar must abide with program-related requirements aside from those stipulated above such as but not limited to attendance in fellowship activities and other out-of-school activities that may be organized for scholars, reporting of grades, monitoring and evaluation of academic performance and voluntary service.
